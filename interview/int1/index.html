为保护受访者隐私，部分敏感信息已脱敏

疫情前的生活基本上是三点一线。孩子可以正常上学，早晨起来送完他，我就去公司了。到公司里边忙一天，下午我不用接他，晚上回家。一日三餐中一般中午我不在家吃，其他时间都是在家就餐的。

我是做鞋和服装生意的，在奥特莱斯的生意不算大，但要比打工强一些。疫情开始之前，生意已经不像前几年那么景气了，但能正常运转。我们卖的鞋一年大概分三季——春夏秋冬本来是四季，但春天很短，基本都是穿凉鞋过渡了。每批的订货量一个季节下来的话，大概四五十万件。靴子的单价比单鞋高，所以它的对比会递增一些。每一批货里还会有中途的补货。如果一款鞋卖得好，我们就会继续跟单，有时候会跟一两年。

疫情是19年底开始的嘛，我本来要跟朋友出国玩。我那个朋友在医院工作，他给我打了个电话，说你尽量别出门，最近的感冒特别严重，医院开会说致死率百分之十几。后来他去泰国了，我没去。等到阴历年底，春节前，我听说这个病叫“新冠病毒”。新闻里说得挺严重，但我自己心里感觉没那么严重。当时要求居家，孩子刚好在放假，我们就不出门了，备了一些菜、米、面、油之类的，买的面差不多得够一年吃的了。

我们店的销售任务是商场定的：集团给各个商场下任务，商场把这个任务下到每个商家头上，根据之前的销售量来定。销售任务按平米算，我是每平米9500块的任务，我的店有30平米，那用30乘以9500，就是总任务量。疫情刚开始的时候，国家有政策，减免了50%的社保费用，商场对我们商户（销售任务）也会有减免。你想想，从一个月能卖三十万到一个月卖九千块，是不是落差很大呀？减免到2020年八月份，八月底九月初就又开始有新任务了。可是任务根本完不成：全国疫情不停地发散，我们去不了广州上货了，因为坐飞机都需要48小时内的核酸证明，到了那边如果有疫情还可能要在当地隔离，所以只能打视频沟通上货，用快递发到北京。北京这边也随时都可能有疫情，商场就算不关门，人也特别少，上过来的货会堆销不出去。扛了一年多，这疫情一直不过去，感觉越来越难了。所以今年我和YS奥特莱斯的合同3月31号到期，我就不再续签，撤出来了，基本上属于失业状态。

在我们撤出之前，已经有很多人都撤了，商场有好多空摊位。好多公司不是北京当地的，货都要快递过来，员工也不好找。外地人在北京生活需要租房，以前在商场里边挣得多，比如一个月挣七八千，租房子花一千多，还能生存。现在他们只能拿个四五千块钱，再花两千块钱租房，还要吃饭，就生存不下去了。他们只能回老家，陪陪孩子什么的。疫情期间物价上升，大家都在想，真是一天一个价啊。我的合同结束后，店里的导购员一共十几个人，大多数都已经找了新工作：有些（外地）人选择留在北京，不想回老家，就在商场另找了职位。还没找工作的基本都是北京本地人，因为感觉出去不安全，就准备先在家休息一段时间再说。

我认为受影响最大的是那些打工族，好多人经过疫情都已经失业了。比如餐饮不让开了，一关就是几个月，服务员就都得回家。我们YS奥特莱斯开业22年，不管是疫情的第一年还是什么时间，从来没有停过业——大年三十开到晚上六点，大年初一、初二开到八点，其他日子都是开到十点。结果今年四月份，商场也接到上级领导通知，从4月2号关到了29号。关门之后导购们没有工资，顶多是单位给上个保险。

政府的政策也是为了老百姓好吧，比如疫情扩散时要求（密接者）隔离，我认为是个好事。但居家隔离就可以，集体隔离或者去方舱就没有必要了——大家都在一块儿，会不会互相传染呀？现在倒不用集体隔离了。

如果小区出了一个阳性，夜里小区可能会通知你被封掉了，那你明天早上就出不去了。先给你把门锁锁上，你只能待在自己家，不能下楼，如果十四天后小区里没有其他人得病，你才可以出来。门锁是电磁的，私自打开就会自动报警。如果家里没有吃的可以叫外卖，有专门的志愿者给你送，志愿者可以打开门锁。

2020年十月，因为北京房租太贵，我把库房搬到了燕郊。我每天需要进出京，（这时河北爆发了疫情吗？）有一天大数据捕捉到我，我（健康宝）就被弹窗了。我给社区打电话，（社区）说需要居家隔离，我就自己在家隔离了14天，我爱人、婆婆、阿姨去他弟弟家住了（“我婆婆和阿姨就跟我爱人回他弟弟家住了”？）。今年从三月份到现在我都没去河北——去不了，因为（孩子的）学校不让出京呀。

都希望能赶快好起来！现在出门随时都受限制。你看，前几天因为确诊病例去过朝阳区天堂酒吧，我们又做了三轮核酸，一轮三天，一下做了九天。公司里的小郭在燕郊连做了二十一天，每天“被捅”，后来他实在受不了，就开车回河南了。北京鲜奶保质期还48小时呢，人的保质期都不如鲜奶长。核酸已经常态化了，每个小区（走路）十几分钟就会有一个核酸点。基本上都是免费做，不赔。我们小区那儿很快，如果大家集体做核酸的话，差不多有八个点开着，跟着其他人往前走，十来分钟就完事了。扫码，刷身份证，做核酸，很快很快的，不像以前排大长队。

回到之前的生活状态不太可能吧。现在大环境不好，消费和挣来的钱不成比例。还有……怎么说呢？现在大家都很难，我感觉年轻人还不如老年人呢。老年人的退休金一个月一万多，这年轻人拼死累活也挣不了四五千块。以后年轻人会越来越难吧。疫情刚开始时我觉得几个月就会过去，结果之后一点一点演变，我想着要研发疫苗怎么也得需要两年，然后疫情才能结束。可现在已经两年多了。如果解决不了，以后就可能会常态化了。中国现在是有疫苗可打了，之后不是一直在打加强针吗？打完第一针过六个月打第二针，几个月后再打第三针，现在说是要准备再打第四针呢，不知道什么时候打。如果几年之内能研发出来打一针就够的疫苗，大家可能就没事了，或者（得了新冠的话）吃点感冒药就过去了。

English Interview Record

To protect the interviewee's privacy, certain information has been desensitized.

Before COVID, my life was quite static. I would send him off to school, and go to the company for the rest of the day. Since I didn’t have to pick him up in the afternoon, I normally don’t eat at home, and only goes back at night.

My business is around shoes and clothing. Even though my business is not considered big in outlets, it’s still somewhat better than working for others. We order our shoes by season, separated by summer, fall, and winter. Since spring is very short, we usually use sandals to pass through the season. Usually, we order about 400,000 to 500,000 pieces every season, and if a product sells very well, we would make supplementary purchases in the middle of the season, sometimes for a year of two.

COVID started at the end of 2019. I was originally going to travel abroad with friends, but another friend working at a hospital called me, and advised against it. He said that recent colds are very severe, that the death rate is around 10%. Later, he went to Thailand, and I didn’t. Around Lunar New Year, I heard of this COVID-19. The news portrayed it as very severe, but I didn’t think of it so. When we were required to stay home, my kids were in vacation too, si we didn’t leave, and prepared vegetables, rice, and oil sufficient for around a year.

The supermarket provides us with sales target. The corporation gives each supermarket its sales targets, and the supermarkets divide the target amount down to each individual merchant. Previously, sale targets are calculated by space taken up by the merchant. I had a goal of ¥9500 rmb per meter squared, and since my store takes up 30 meters squared, my total sales target is 30 times 9500. When COVID just broke out, there was national policies which decreased 50% of social security costs, and our sale targets too. We suddenly went from being able to sell 300,000 monthly to 9000. The policies lasted until around August of 2020, and new targets were set for late August and September. However, they were impossible to accomplish. Since COVID was spreading nationwide, we could not order from Guangzhou directly. Taking a plane required a negative COVID test within the last 48 ours, and if COVID broke out in Guangzhou, we had to quarantine there. We were thus forced to order through video call, and have it shipped to Beijing. There could be a COVID outbreak anytime in Beijing too. Even when the supermarket didn’t close, there were very few people, and stockpiles struggle to sell. After struggling for a year, as the light at the end of the tunnel is still struggling to be seen, I stopped renewing my contrast with Yansha Outlets on March 31st, and drew out. Now, I’m practically unemployed.

Many others withdrew before we did, and there were a lot of empty spots in the supermarket. Many companies are not local from Beijing, so they had to import everything. It has become difficult to find employees too. People from outside Beijing had to rent houses. Previously, one can earn a lot in the supermarket, something around seven or eight thousand, and since rent is around one thousand, they can sustain themselves. Now, they can only earn four or five thousand monthly, and rent has increased too, to perhaps two thousand. That has now become unsustainable. Now, they can only return to their hometown. Inflation is also severe during COVID, the prices literately increase every day. When I ended my contrast, there was around ten employees. Some foreigners wanted to stay at Beijing, so they found new positions in the supermarket. The ones who did not find new positions are mostly locals, since they feel unsafe, and thus decided to stay at home for a while.

I think those affected most are the working people. A lot of restaurants closed for several months, so most employees had to go home. Our outlets has opened for 22 years, and has never closed, whether when COVID broke out, or on Chinese New Year. But in April of this year, we’ve also received orders to close from April 2nd to 29th, during which no employees received any compensations, at most insurance from the employer.

I do think governmental policies are based on good intentions, for the good of the people. Contact-tracing policies, and requirements for those with close-contact to quarantine are effective. However, I think self-quarantine at home is sufficient, and concentrated quarantine is unnecessary, since it would also exasperate cross-contact between all those quarantined.

If there’s a positive case in a community, it might be locked down overnight, and no one would be able to leave starting the next day. They would come and lock the door from the outside, so you can only stay at your room and be unable to leave. If no one else tests positive in 14 days, then the quarantine would be lifted. The lock is electric, so if you open the door by yourself, an alarm would sound. If there’s no food at home and you have to call delivery, there would be volunteers who deliver, and they are able to unlock the door.

In October of 2020, due to the rising rent in Beijing, I moved our storage to Yanjiao in Hubei. One day, detected by big data, a Pop-up window indicating potential exposure to COVID appeared in my QR code. So, I called my community telling them I had to quarantine, and stayed at home for 14 days. In the meantime, my husband, mother-in-law, and house-keeping moved to my husband’s brother’s place. I haven’t been able to go to Hebei since March, since my kid’s school do not permit students leaving Beijing.

I hope that everything will return to normal soon! Now, there’s always restrictions on even leaving the house. Several days ago, due to an outbreak centered in Chaoyang district, we had to do three more rounds of COVID tests. Even round was three days, so it took a total of nine days. One of my coworkers had to take a COVID test every day for 21 days. In the end, he could not endure it any further, so drove back to Henan. Even milk have an expiration period of 48 hour, and our COVID validation period isn’t as long as milk. COVID tests are a norm now, there would be a COVID test site every ten or so minute by walk. Tests are for free, and convenient in my community. If everyone are required to take a COVID test, there would be around eight sites open, and would take an average of around ten minutes. The process is just to scan the QR code, the ID, and take the test. It has become much more convenient than before, where everyone had to wait in long lines.

I don’t think I can return to the living style as before. The large social system has changed now for the worse. Spending and earning are no longer compatible with each other. Everyone are struggling now, and I think younger people are struggling more than even the older folks. The retirement compensation is more than 10,000 a month, and young people struggle to make four or five thousand. Perhaps the lives of younger people will only become more and more difficult. In the beginning of the pandemic, I thought it would be over in several months. I thought vaccine development would take around two years, and then the pandemic would be over. Now, it has been more than two years. If a zero-COVID policy cannot solve the issue, we might really have to live with it. Now there’s vaccines in China, and boosters once in a while. The second shot is six months following the first, and the third several months after the second. Now, there are rumors that there would be a fourth shot, God knows when that will be. If there can be a vaccine that defends everyone with one shot, or perhaps makes symptoms as mild as a cold perhaps everyone would be fine.
